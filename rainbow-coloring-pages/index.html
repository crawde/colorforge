Coloring Tips for Rainbow Pages

Always color in order

Start from the top (or outside) arc and work inward: red → orange → yellow → green → blue → indigo → violet. This prevents smudging and teaches kids the correct rainbow color order. Sing ROY G BIV as you go!

Blend between stripes

For a realistic gradient rainbow, overlap colors slightly where they meet — press lightly with each color at the boundary so they blend. Red transitions to orange at the edge, orange transitions to yellow, etc. Creates a smooth, natural-looking spectrum.

Add sparkle effects

After coloring, add white crayon or gel pen dots along the rainbow arcs for a sparkle/dewdrop effect. Scatter tiny star shapes around the rainbow. These small additions make the rainbow feel magical instead of just scientific.

Cloud texture technique

Don't leave clouds white — add light gray shadows on the bottom and sides of each cloud puff. Use a light blue pencil very gently along cloud edges. This gives clouds dimension and makes the rainbow appear to emerge FROM them.

Activity Ideas

Rainbow Color Sorting

Print rainbow pages alongside a bucket of crayons, markers, or colored objects. Kids sort their supplies into rainbow order before coloring. Reinforces color recognition, ordering, and the ROY G BIV sequence in a hands-on way.

Rainbow Week Challenge

Generate 7 rainbow pages — one for each day. Each day, kids only color ONE stripe of every rainbow (Monday = red, Tuesday = orange...). By Friday, the rainbows are complete! Teaches patience, sequence, and makes a week-long activity.

DIY Rainbow Room Banner

Print 7 identical rainbow pages on cardstock. Color each one predominantly in a single color (monochrome red, monochrome orange, etc.). String them together to create a rainbow banner across the room. Art meets decoration.

Science + Art Rainbow

After coloring rainbow pages, make a real rainbow: hold a glass of water in a sunbeam and catch the prism on white paper. Or use a garden hose spray in sunlight. Compare the real rainbow colors to what you drew. Science meets art.
