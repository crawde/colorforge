Dinosaur Facts for Colorists

🦕 Tyrannosaurus Rex

The T-Rex had teeth the size of bananas — up to 12 inches long. When coloring a T-Rex, add detail to those massive jaws! Use dark greens, browns, and grays for a realistic look.

🦕 Triceratops

Triceratops had over 400-800 teeth arranged in groups called 'batteries.' Their distinctive three-horned frill makes them one of the most recognizable and fun dinosaurs to color.

🦕 Brachiosaurus

Brachiosaurus could reach heights of 40+ feet — taller than a 4-story building. Their long necks make for dramatic coloring pages. Try light greens and tans for a natural look.

🦕 Velociraptor

Real velociraptors were only about 2 feet tall — nothing like the movies! They had feathers, which recent paleontology confirms. Advanced colorists can try adding feather detailing.

🦕 Stegosaurus

The plates along a Stegosaurus's back may have been used for temperature regulation and display. Color each plate a different shade for a stunning rainbow effect.

🦕 Pteranodon

Not technically a dinosaur, but a flying reptile! Pteranodon wingspans reached 20+ feet. Their wings and crests create beautiful silhouettes for coloring pages.

Coloring Tips for Dino Pages

Research real dino colors

Scientists have actually determined the colors of some dinosaurs from fossilized pigment cells. Sinosauropteryx was reddish-brown with a striped tail. Anchiornis had black and white wings with a red crest. Use real paleos colors for educational coloring!

Layer green shades for skin

Don't use one flat green. Start with a light sage base, add medium green in the middle tones, and dark forest green in shadows. This three-layer technique makes any dinosaur look three-dimensional.

Use warm colors for the background

Prehistoric Earth was warmer than today. Use warm oranges, yellows, and reds for volcanic landscapes or soft greens and blues for lush Cretaceous jungles to set the mood.

Add your own details

After printing, use a fine-tip pen to add extra details — cracks in dinosaur skin, individual leaves on trees, or tiny insects. This makes the page uniquely yours.
