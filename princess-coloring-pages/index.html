Coloring Tips for Princess Pages

Layer pink shades for gowns

Don't use one flat pink for a princess dress. Start with light blush for highlights, add rose pink for mid-tones, and deep magenta for shadows and folds. This makes the dress look three-dimensional and luxurious.

Add shimmer with metallic crayons

If you have metallic gold or silver colored pencils, use them for tiaras, jewelry, crowns, and scepters. Regular yellow or gray won't capture the royal sparkle. Even a few metallic touches elevate the entire page.

Color the background last

Color the princess first, then fill in the background around her. This prevents smudging the main character and lets you choose complementary background colors once you see how the princess looks.

Use complementary colors

If the princess wears a purple gown, use gold/yellow for the crown and accessories. Blue dress? Try orange or coral accents. Complementary colors (opposite on the color wheel) make royal designs pop dramatically.
