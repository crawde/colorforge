Coloring Tips for Dragon Pages

Scale-by-scale for realism

Don't color a dragon's body in one solid shade. Color individual scales with slight color variations — dark green on top of each scale, lighter green at the bottom. This creates a 3D, textured look that makes the dragon feel alive.

Make fire glow with layering

Dragon fire should glow: start with white/yellow at the center (closest to the mouth), transition to orange in the middle, and finish with red/dark red at the flame tips. Leave white spots uncolored in the center for brightness.

Use metallic pencils for treasure

If you have metallic colored pencils (gold, silver, copper), save them for the treasure hoard, crown, and armored scales. The contrast between metallic accents and regular coloring makes the dragon page look premium.

Background contrast matters

Dark dragons pop against light backgrounds (sunset skies, snow fields). Light dragons pop against dark backgrounds (night sky, deep caves). Choose your background after coloring the dragon so colors complement rather than compete.
