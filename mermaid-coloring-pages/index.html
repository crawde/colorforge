Coloring Tips for Mermaid Pages

Create iridescent scales

For a realistic mermaid tail, don't use one flat color. Color individual scales in gradients — light teal at the top fading to deep emerald at the bottom. Alternate scale rows with slightly different hues (blue-green, purple-blue) for a shimmery, iridescent effect.

Use watercolors for ocean backgrounds

Print the page on thick paper and use watercolors for the ocean background. The soft, flowing watercolor washes create a perfect underwater atmosphere. Color the mermaid with colored pencils on top for crisp detail against the dreamy background.

Add sparkle with gel pens

After coloring with pencils or crayons, add gel pen details — white dots on scales for shimmer, silver swirls in hair for water reflections, and gold outlines on seashell accessories. Metallic gel pens make coral and treasure glow.

Blend ocean depth gradients

Color the water from light turquoise at the surface to deep navy at the bottom. This creates realistic ocean depth. The mermaid at the center should have the most vibrant colors, making her pop against the gradient background.
