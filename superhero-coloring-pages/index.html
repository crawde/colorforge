Coloring Tips for Hero Pages

Use bold, saturated colors

Superheroes demand bold colors — not pastels. Press harder with your pencils for rich reds, deep blues, and vivid yellows. Comic book heroes are all about high contrast and visual impact. Save subtle coloring for other genres.

Add action lines with markers

After coloring the hero, use thin markers or fine-tip pens to add speed lines, impact bursts, and energy trails. Short lines radiating from a punch = impact. Curved lines behind a flying hero = speed. These small details make pages feel alive.

Use complementary color schemes

Classic hero costumes use complementary colors: red & blue, purple & yellow, green & red. These opposites on the color wheel create maximum visual contrast and make heroes look iconic. Choose your scheme before coloring.

Leave white highlights for shine

Don't color every inch. Leave small white spots on masks, boots, and metal surfaces for highlights and shine. This creates a 3D, glossy comic-book effect that makes the hero look polished and powerful.
