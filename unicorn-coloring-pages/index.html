Coloring Tips for Unicorn Pages

Start with the mane and tail

The flowing hair is the most fun part of a unicorn design. Use rainbow gradients (ROYGBIV order) or complementary color pairs. Colored pencils give the best blending for gradient manes.

Use metallic or glitter for the horn

After coloring, add a line of glitter glue along the horn spiral. Gold, silver, or holographic glitter transforms a flat coloring page into something magical. Let dry 2 hours before framing.

Background color matters

A light purple or blue background behind a unicorn makes white/pastel colors pop. Use a single soft pastel across the entire background for a professional look without overpowering the subject.

Try different media on different sections

Markers for the body (bold flat color), colored pencils for the mane (blendable gradients), and gel pens for small details (stars, flowers, horn). Mixing media adds visual interest.

What's the best paper for printing coloring pages?

For crayons and colored pencils: 24-28lb paper (standard printer paper works). For markers: 32lb+ paper or cardstock to prevent bleed-through. For display/framing: 65-110lb cardstock. Laser printers produce crisper lines than inkjet for coloring pages.
